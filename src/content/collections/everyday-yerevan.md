Yerevan is often introduced through its monuments and famous landmarks. Yet much of the city's character exists elsewhere—in quiet courtyards, neighbourhood cafés, apartment entrances, workshops, gardens and streets where everyday life unfolds.

This collection explores those ordinary places that rarely appear in guidebooks but reveal how the city is truly experienced by its residents.

Architecture becomes meaningful through use. A staircase worn by decades of footsteps, a courtyard filled with improvised furniture, or a local market buzzing with conversation all tell stories about community, adaptation and memory.

Looking at these spaces with curiosity reveals a different Yerevan—less monumental perhaps, but deeply human. It is a city shaped not only by architects and planners but also by generations of people who continue to redefine it through daily life.

The Everyday Yerevan collection invites visitors to slow down, observe carefully and appreciate the beauty found in ordinary places.
