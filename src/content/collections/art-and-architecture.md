Architecture and art have always influenced one another. Buildings shape how we experience the city, while artists continually reinterpret those same spaces through observation, intervention and imagination.

As a contemporary artist and curator, I see Yerevan not only as a collection of buildings but as an evolving cultural landscape where architecture, sculpture, design and everyday life constantly interact.

This collection brings together places where artistic practice and architecture meet: museums, public sculptures, exhibition spaces, modernist buildings and unexpected interventions found throughout the city.

Rather than separating architecture from contemporary art, it invites visitors to see both as part of a shared conversation about culture, memory and identity.

Walking through Yerevan becomes an act of looking more carefully—discovering relationships between form, history and artistic expression that often remain unnoticed.
