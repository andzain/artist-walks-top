Constructivism was more than an architectural style. It was an attempt to imagine an entirely new society through design. Buildings became laboratories where ideas about education, work, housing and collective life could be tested.

Although many early constructivist buildings have disappeared or been transformed, their influence continues to shape Yerevan's urban fabric. The city's planning, public institutions and architectural language still carry traces of these experimental decades.

This collection explores the optimism and radical imagination that defined the first years of Soviet Armenia, revealing how architecture became a tool for creating a new way of living.

Understanding Constructivism also helps us understand everything that followed—from the monumental modernism of the 1960s to today's debates about preservation and redevelopment.
