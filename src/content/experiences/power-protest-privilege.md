# Power, Protest & Privilege

## A Political Walk Through Modern Yerevan

Every city tells a story of power.

In Yerevan, that story is written into its squares, monuments, government buildings and streets. History is not confined to museums—it continues to shape the city we experience today.

This walk invites you to discover modern Armenia through the places where political life unfolded and continues to evolve.

---

## Why This Walk?

This is not a chronological history tour.

Nor is it a political lecture.

Instead, we read Yerevan itself as a political landscape. Architecture, public space and monuments become documents that reveal changing relationships between power, memory and society.

By the end of the walk, you'll begin to see the city differently.

---

# The Walk

## Republic Square

The symbolic heart of Armenia.

Here we explore Alexander Tamanyan's vision for the capital, Soviet monumental architecture, and the transformation of Republic Square into the country's principal stage for public life.

From official ceremonies to the Velvet Revolution, few places have witnessed more of Armenia's modern history.

---

## Freedom Square

For decades this has been the city's public forum.

Standing here, we discuss demonstrations, civil resistance and the relationship between urban space and political change. It is one of the places where Armenian society has repeatedly gathered to make its voice heard.

---

## Northern Avenue

Elegant on the surface.

Controversial beneath it.

Northern Avenue tells the story of post-Soviet redevelopment, privatization and the emergence of a new economic elite. It offers a striking example of how political and economic decisions reshape the fabric of a city.

---

## Monuments & Memory

Cities remember selectively.

Every monument celebrates one story while leaving another untold.

Together we explore how public monuments, memorials and symbols reflect changing political narratives and evolving ideas of national identity.

---

## The Living City

History did not end with independence or the Velvet Revolution.

Throughout the walk we connect historical events with contemporary Armenia, exploring how the past continues to influence everyday political and social life.

---

# What You'll Take Away

This experience combines

- political history
- architecture
- urban planning
- public space
- contemporary culture
- social change

Rather than offering simple answers, the walk encourages careful observation, discussion and curiosity.

You'll leave with a deeper understanding of modern Armenia—and a new way of looking at Yerevan itself.
