# The Cascade Code: Soviet Dreams and Contemporary Art

## The Story of a "City of Sun"

Our walk begins at the feet of Alexander Tamanyan, the man who "invented" modern Yerevan. We will examine the stone map of his 1924 Master Plan to understand how he transformed a provincial town into a neoclassical "Solar City." You will learn why the architect is depicted leaning over his work and how his vision still dictates the rhythm of our lives today.

## A Museum Under the Open Sky

As we move into the Cafesjian Sculpture Garden, the atmosphere shifts from history to global contemporary art. We will walk among the world-famous works of Fernando Botero, Barry Flanagan, and Jaume Plensa. I will help you "read" these sculptures and explain how a private collection from America turned a Soviet construction site into one of the world's most unique outdoor museums.

## The Architecture of the Infinite

Next, we ascend the Cascade. I will tell you the dramatic 40-year history of this "stairway to heaven"—from its inception in the 1970s as a Soviet monument to its current status as a cultural center. We will reach the upper platform to stand before the "Reborn Armenia" Obelisk. Built in 1967, this 50-meter monument marks the geometric center of Tamanyan's axis and symbolizes the resilience of the Armenian spirit.

## The Descent: Yerevan at Your Feet

We will conclude our hour-long journey by descending the grand staircase (or escalators), enjoying the most iconic panoramic view of the city and Mount Ararat. By the time we reach the base, you will see Yerevan not just as a city of buildings, but as a living, breathing work of art.
